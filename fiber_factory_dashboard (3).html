How this system works

Fiber is produced from banana trunks and dried → packed into sacks → the fiber is turned into paper → the paper is dried and packed into sacks → shipped out to make sanitary pads. Every batch and sack gets a unique ID. The rule that matters: first prepared, first used — the oldest fiber batch must be used before a newer one, and the same for sacks and paper batches. This page gives you the full picture: what's been produced, what's been used, and what still needs attention.
